Prayer times use the calculation method you pick above (angles per that authority), with Asr by your selected madhab. Everything runs on-device from GPS alone — no internet or SIM needed, even mid-flight. Clock times use an estimated local zone from GPS longitude (nearest half-hour), not your phone's home time zone, so they stay meaningful as you cross countries with no signal. This can be off by up to ~1 hour in a few regions where borders don't follow longitude (e.g. China, Nepal). In-flight direction uses the GPS ground track (true north); in a crosswind the aircraft's nose can differ from the track by a few degrees (crab angle), so treat the needle as accurate to roughly ±5–10° while flying. On the ground when stationary, the phone's compass takes over — its magnetic reading is corrected to true north using an on-device World Magnetic Model, so the remaining error is mainly local magnetic interference (nearby metal, speakers, magnets), not declination. The Hijri date and the calendar at the bottom both use an on-device calendar calculation, not local moon-sighting, so they can be off by a day or two from an official announcement — treat them as a guide, not an authority.
